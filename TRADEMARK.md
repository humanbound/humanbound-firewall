## What you may do

- State factually that your project "uses Humanbound" or is "compatible with
  Humanbound" where that is accurate
- Use the Humanbound name in technical documentation to refer to the software
  or the company
- Use unmodified Humanbound logos with a link back to [humanbound.ai](https://humanbound.ai)
  in conference talks, academic papers, blog posts, or educational material,
  provided your use does not imply official endorsement or sponsorship

## What you may not do without written permission

- Use the Humanbound name, logo, or any confusingly similar mark as the name
  of your own product, service, company, project, or domain
- Modify, distort, recolor, or incorporate the Humanbound logo into derived
  logos
- Suggest or imply official endorsement, partnership, certification, or
  sponsorship by Humanbound without a signed agreement
- Distribute a modified version of `humanbound-firewall` under the Humanbound
  name (a fork must be renamed — Apache-2.0 permits the fork, but the trademark
  does not permit the name)

## Permitted nominative use

These uses do not require permission:

- Referring to Humanbound software in a package dependency list (`humanbound-firewall`
  in `requirements.txt` is fine)
- Naming Humanbound in a comparison table or benchmark result (provided the
  comparison is truthful and not misleading)
- Academic citation of Humanbound research or software

## Enforcement

Humanbound takes trademark protection seriously because it protects our users
from confusion about what is and isn't an official Humanbound product. If you
believe a use of the Humanbound name or logo is infringing, or you are unsure
whether your planned use is permitted, contact
[legal@humanbound.ai](mailto:legal@humanbound.ai).
